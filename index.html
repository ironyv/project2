About Us

At WUGI We Strive to Fufill 4 Goals

1. Make routine trips to Guatemala to repair equipment in Roosevelt Hospital

2. Teach hospital staff to repair and maintain various types of medical equipment

3. Fundraise to purchase and send necessary equipment and tools to Roosevelt Hospital

4. Work and maintain a bilateral relationship with La Universidad del Valle (UVG), a prominent engineering school in Guatemala

Background

Quick Facts

  • Most Populous County in Central America
    • 15,428,834 People
  • Official Languge: Spanish
    • 21 other indigenous (Mayan) languages spoken
  • Virtually void of a middle class
  • Over half of the population lives in rural areas
  • Over half of the population lives in poverty
  • 75% of indigenous people are impoverished
  • Child Labor Prevalent in poor areas
  • High risk for major infectious diseases
    • Bacterial diarrhea, hepatitis A, typhoid fever, dengue fever and malaria

A Brief History of ALAS

  • 16th Century: colonized by Spain
  • 1821 Century: Gained Independence from Spain
  • 1898-1920 Dictatorship
  • 1944-1996 52 Year Civil War
    • conflict rooted in inequalities in economic and political life
    • guerrilla warfare
    • 1981-1983 genocide in Guatemala
    • Armed conflict ended with a peace treaty

Why Guatemala Needs WUGI

  • 40% of the population receives no access to healthcare
  • 1 hospital bed per 1,000 people
  • Rural residents live hours away from a hospital
  • Public hospitals are only funded by 2% of GDP
    • Ambulances are not readily available for the general population
    • Hospitals are void of proper imaging equipment
    • Hospitals are void of basic and vital medicines
    • Equipment that is prevalent is most likely broken
