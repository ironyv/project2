Welcome! ¡Bienvenidos! Bem-vindo! We are a student organization dedicated to bringing diverse groups of students together. We have been celebrating 23 years of culture, community, and family. To learn more click the button below!

About Us

At ALAS, we hold the inclusion and respect of different cultures paramount in achieving a well-rounded education. While we are titled "Association of Latin American Students", we encourage anyone and everyone to come to one of our general body meetings to at least learn more about our organization and to see that we are by no means made up of only latino/a students. We believe that every individual has something important to contribute and hope that ALAS can serve as a place for anyone to openly express themselves. Partnered programs include:

1. Casa De Salud: A health and wellness center for the immigrants of St. Louis

2. Niños Cambios Puertas: Tutoring and mentoring Latino/a youth in St. Louis

3. Hispanic Chamber of Commerce of Metropolitan St. Louis: Provide support for small hispanic businesses

Quick Facts

  • Latin America is the most urbanized region in the world
    • 80% of its population lives in cities
  • Main Official Languge Spoken: Spanish and Portuguese
    • Over 200 other indigenous languages spoken
  • The Amazon sprawls across eight countries, comprises 1.4 billion acres of dense forest and is home to one in ten of all known species on earth (WWF) – as well as plenty of the unknown ones
  • More than half of Latin American workers do not have access to a pension through their job (OECD)
  • More than 20 million Latin American and Caribbean people live outside their country of birth; some three quarters are in the United States and many of the rest in Argentina, Venezuela, Spain and Canada (OECD)
  • Asia is Latin America and the Caribbean’s second largest trading partner after the US. As a result of Asia’s demand for commodities – from iron ore to soy beans – trade between Asia and Latin America/the Caribbean grew at over 20% a year in the past decade, reaching US$ 442 billion in 2011 (ADB)
  • GDP in Latin America and the Caribbean is predicted to grow by 3.6% in 2013, picking up from 3% in 2012. This compares with a projected growth rate of 2% in the United States and 7.1% in developing Asian countries (IMF)

Carnaval

  • One of the largest shows on WashU's campus, Carnaval is a cultural show that celebrates several popular cultural dances while highlighting Latin-American issues with the use of a skit
  • Occurs around the time of Carnaval in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
    • Largest street-festival in the world, Carnaval is celebrated around the time of Mardi Gras
    • Several Samba dance groups prepare year-round and create several extravagant floats
  • Students from all groups and disciplines are encouraged to become active in Carnaval, and most participants are not members of ALAS
